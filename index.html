Reading & Writing

Scheduling enrichment

Books

The Magic Mountain
Thomas Mann

Started this 6/19. Only a chapter in but im so hooked. Mann published this in 1924 as he began to reckon with fascism and the consequences of the nationalism that spurred wwi. The story follows Hans Castorp, a 20-year-old from Hamburg who is visiting his cousin Joachim in a sanitorium in the Swiss alps. I had to read it after Austria bc the setting of salzburg is fairly similar. The writing is just so gripping and leaves the reader with much to consider.

The Human Stain
Philip Roth

Finished this 6/16. Maybe the best thing i've read all year... It's about a professor at a small liberal arts college in western mass (hello Smith) who gets basically canceled over a questionable offense and his life kind of crumbles. it's so funny, so far-reaching (I think this is really a book about the condition of the late 20th century in America and how history haunts us all) and so engaging.

The Fire Next Time
James Baldwin

Finished 6/17 - A set of letters Baldwin wrote to his nephew in 1963. Really prescient and wonderful to read. Baldwin is a genius and reading him is always like a shock of clarity. This was also quick but had a lot of great lines and passages that i will be returning to.

A Favourite of the Gods
Sybille Bedford

Finished 6/11 - wonderful intergenerational family saga set in Italy and England -- interesting thoughts on generational feminism and the condition of women more generally. I'm more looking forward to reading the follow-up novel which follows the youngest daughter's lesbian pursuits.

I Have Some Questions For You
Rebecca Makkai

Finished 6/8 - plane/ferry read. Set at a boarding school in NH which was fun considering the boarding-school turn my life is about to take. i thought this was a good sort of mystery with some more overarching questions about sexual violence, true crime, and the power of spectacle in cultural processing of these traumas. I don't read a ton of contemporary fiction like this but it didn't TOTALLY feel like junk food but was still engaging and easy to get through.

Yesteryear
Caro Claire Burke

Finished 5/30 - was having fun reading this in Austria when i couldn't sleep bc of jet lag. Wasn't totally thrilled with the ending- or the writing to be honest which felt cheap and stupid-- but i think the concept was good and I'm glad that it exists I guess? It was funny and biting and good at points but I don't know that I'd recommend it.

In the Woods
Tana French

Finished 5/25 - i LOVE my tana french irish mysteries and they are great during the semester when I can't take on anything that is actually intellectually challenging outside of class. This was good and fun and gripping. Tana French knows how to keep u on ur toes but also makes you work for it if that makes sense. the prose is good, not masterful or anything, but still worth reading.

Chess Story
Stefan Zweig

Finished 5/24 - This is a wonderful and brilliant little book about a prisoner of war and a Hungarian chess prodigy. Zweig published this in midst of-- but before conclusion of-- WWII, before the mass devastation that occurred was public knowledge I found it really striking and really memorable. I wanted to read Zweig in advance of my trip to Austria bc he spent a lot of time in Salzburg and ultimately am really glad that I picked this one up.
